Snapping

Snapping allows transformations to align precisely to existing geometry such as vertices, edges, or faces.

Snapping is applied while transforming using Move, Rotate, Scale, or the Transform Gizmo.

How to Activate Snapping

  • In the viewport, at the middle-top, there is a magnet button.
  • Press the magnet button to enable snapping; press again to disable snapping.
  • Next to the magnet button, select the Snap Target Type: Vertex, Edge, or Face.

Snap Targets

  • Vertex Snap — snaps to the nearest visible vertex.
  • Edge Snap — snaps to the closest point along an edge.
  • Face Snap — snaps to a surface point on a face.

How Snapping Works

  • Snapping is evaluated in screen space, based on the mouse cursor position.
  • The closest valid snap target within the snap threshold is selected.
  • A visual indicator appears when a valid snap target is detected.

Transform-Specific Behavior

Move (Translate)

  • The selection is translated so the nearest original vertex aligns with the snap target.
  • The entire selection moves together as a group.
  • Axis constraints are respected during snapping.

Rotate

  • The selection is rotated toward the snap target around the transform pivot.
  • When an axis is constrained, rotation is limited to that axis.
  • Without an axis constraint, the rotation aligns the direction from the pivot to the snap target.

Scale

  • Scaling adjusts so the selection reaches the snap target.
  • Scale snapping is computed relative to the transform pivot.
  • Axis constraints limit scaling to specific directions.

Edit Mode Snapping

  • Snapping affects only the selected vertices, edges, or faces.
  • Selected vertices are ignored as snap targets to prevent self-snapping.
  • The shape of the selection is always preserved.

Axis and Space

  • Snapping respects axis constraints (X, Y, Z).
  • In World Space, snapping aligns using global axes.
  • In Local Space, snapping respects object rotation.

Visual Feedback

  • A circular snap indicator appears at the snap location.
  • The indicator scales with camera distance to remain readable.

Snapping works together with transform orientation, axis constraints, and gizmo controls to provide precise modeling workflows.
