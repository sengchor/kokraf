Viewport Shading

Viewport Shading controls how objects in the scene are visually displayed in the 3D viewport. It allows you to preview your models in different shading styles for modeling, texturing, or debugging purposes.

Viewport Shading does not affect the final render. It is only for visual feedback in the editor.

Location

  • The Viewport Shading menu is located at the top-right corner of the viewport.
  • It can be changed by clicking the shading icon and selecting one of the available shading modes.

Shading Modes

  • Material — displays objects with their assigned materials and textures. Lighting in the scene is applied only in Material shading mode, allowing realistic previews of colors, textures, and lights.
  • Solid — displays objects using a flat shaded material, helpful for modeling without textures. Uses a default gray color with MatCap lighting.
  • Normal — displays objects using vertex normals for a color-coded visualization of surface directions.
  • Wireframe — displays only the mesh edges, useful for analyzing geometry structure and topology.

Behavior

  • Changing the shading mode updates all objects in the main scene.
  • Shading also affects how helper objects, lights, and cameras are displayed in the viewport.
  • Switching to Solid or Normal mode automatically applies a preview material for easier visualization.

Use different shading modes to speed up your workflow: Solid for modeling, Material for texture preview, Normal for surface inspection, and Wireframe for topology analysis.
